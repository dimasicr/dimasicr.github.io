Geometry of Batik

Sierpinski Tumpal

Description

This design is inspired by the traditional Indonesian Tumpal motive, which is tiled using {3,6} tessellation. Each individual triangle is adorned with a wave-like pattern known as "isen." This intricate wave pattern is crafted using a combination of cubic Bezier curves and spirals. Specifically, the spiral component is designed in such a way that the curve is tangent to the perimeter of the triangle, adding a dynamic and harmonious element to the overall motif.

Math Concept

This piece employs six triangles, rotated around their vertices to form a hexagonal grid. The grid is subsequently filled with a wave-like pattern of isen. This wave-like isen is crafted through a combination of a spiral and a cubic Bezier curve. The spiral is meticulously arranged to be tangent to the perimeter of each triangle. The equation defining the center of the spiral can be expressed as:

\( x_c = 0.5 w - \frac{R}{tan(\theta/2)} \)

\( y_c = R \)

How to Draw

  1. Given triangle ABC, draw an angular bisector line that divides angle α into two equal parts

  2. Draw a semicircle centered on any point of the angular bisector line. The radius of the semicircle is the distance between the points and the base.

  3. Draw another semicircle centered at the same X-coordinate but half of the Y-coordinate. The radius should be half of the previous semicircle.

  4. Draw a curvy wave to the origin
