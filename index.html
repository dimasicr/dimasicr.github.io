Apollonian Kawung

Description

Motive inspired by traditional Indonesian motive Kawung decorated with traditional isen cecek, arranged as Apollonian Gasket. Apollonian Gasket is a circle fractals, where 4 circles is kissing (mutually tangent). The equation to draw this is first described by French philosopher and mathematician, Rene Descartes.

Both Kawung and Apollonian gasket are one of the oldest pattern known. In Indonesia, Kawung motive is found in 13th century status of Mahakala, a relic from Singosari Kingdom. In French, Rene Descartes famous philosopher and mathematician states that four every four kissing circles, the radii of the circles satisfy a certain quadratic equation. We can treat Kawung as a Circle, and by doing that we can use Descartes theorem to draw Apollonian Kawung Gasket

Math Concept

Descartes theorem states that curvature k ( 1 / radius) of four mutually tangent circles can be described as:

\( (k_1 + k_2 + k_3 + k_4)^2 = 2 \cdot (2k_1 ^2 + k_2 ^2+ k_3 ^2 + k_4 ^2) \)

To get location of circle center, we can use complex Descartes theorem:

\( (k_1 z_1 + k_2 z_2 + k_3 z_3 + k_4 z_4)^2 = 2 \cdot (2k_1 ^2 z_1 ^ 2+ k_2 ^2 z_2^2+ k_3 ^2 z_3 ^ 2 + k_4 ^2 z_4 ^ 2) \)

How to draw Kawung

Given a square ABCD with center at O, we can draw circle inscribed inside the square.

We then draw a quarter circle, centered at A. We repeat the same process 3 times for each vertex
